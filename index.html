Why
Starting Point

Why I'm Working
on This

In the past years I have been embedded in different collaborative research ecosystems — most concretely in WP2 of Healthy Living as a Service and through the development of ATMOS_FERA in dialogue with scientific datasets and researchers.

What is the structural role of artistic research inside these kinds of collaborations — beyond producing outputs or communicating results?

↳ Starting point of the FORUM+ article in development.

Epistemic
Core Question

What Does Artistic Research Do —
Structurally?

Rather than asking whether artistic research is valuable, I want to ask how it contributes structurally to collaborative knowledge-making.

How does artistic research reorganize the epistemic conditions of collaboration inside transdisciplinary environments?

By epistemic conditions, I mean
How problems are framed
What counts as evidence
How knowledge is negotiated
How authority is distributed

↳ This moves beyond output and impact.

Claim
Working Claim

Provisional
Argument

Through these cases, I am exploring the argument that artistic research contributes not only through outputs, but by reorganizing how knowledge is framed, experienced, and negotiated within transdisciplinary collaborations.

This is not yet a validated framework. It is an argument grounded in embedded practice, now being sharpened through literature review.

↳ Grounded in HLaS + ATMOS_FERA as embedded probes, not definitive proof.

EMI
Analytic Vocabulary

EMI (Epistemic Mediation Indicators) v0.3 —
Working Analytic Vocabulary

A practitioner-facing analytic vocabulary for tracing how artistic research contributes from within collaboration — not a universal metric, but an exploratory qualitative lens.

01
Problem Reconfiguration
Did the artistic work change how the issue was framed?
02
Knowledge Integration
What kinds of knowledge were brought into relation?
03
Epistemic Position & Authority
Did the intervention alter who could define relevance or interpretation?
04
Experiential Reorientation
Did collaborators come to sense or perceive the issue differently?
05
Evaluation Friction
Where did existing criteria fail to capture what the work was doing?
06
Context & Stage Sensitivity
Under what conditions did these contributions become visible?
Exploratory — failure points as important as usefulness · artistic research evaluation + transdisciplinary epistemic integration literature
Structural
Structural Implication

Why This Matters
Structurally

If artistic research is increasingly included in transdisciplinary collaborations, its epistemic contribution needs to be articulated more clearly.

→
This matters not only for theory, but for how collaborations are designed from the outset.
→
A clearer vocabulary could help define more precisely what artistic research is there to do — from the beginning of a collaboration, not only at its end.
→
The article is a first grounded articulation of this gap — exploratory, not conclusive.
