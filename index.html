Starting Point

Why I Started
Asking This

I have been working inside different collaborative research environments — a health consortium, a collaboration with scientists around planetary data.

In both, I found myself asking: what exactly am I contributing here, epistemically? Not outputs. Not atmosphere. Not outreach. What am I actually doing to how knowledge gets made?

↳ That question is the starting point of the article I'm developing.

Epistemic
Core Question

What Is the Contribution,
Epistemically?

The question is not whether artistic research is valuable. The question is: how does it affect the epistemic conditions of collaboration?

By epistemic conditions, I mean
How problems are framed
What counts as evidence
How knowledge is negotiated
How authority is distributed

↳ This is a different register from impact evaluation.

Claim
Working Claim

My Working
Claim

Artistic research does not only produce outputs. It can reorganize how knowledge is framed, sensed, negotiated, and made meaningful within collaboration.

This is not just an argument about value. It is an argument about the structure of knowledge-making — and about where artistic research sits inside that structure.

↳ Grounded in HLaS and ATMOS_FERA as two concrete collaborative situations.

EMI
A Working Lens

EMI —
Epistemic Mediation Indicators

A qualitative lens and shared vocabulary for naming, positioning, and designing the epistemic role of artistic research inside collaboration. Not a metric. Not a score. A way to ask better questions from the start.

01
Problem Reconfiguration
Did the artistic work change how the problem was framed?
02
Knowledge Integration
What kinds of knowledge were brought into relation?
03
Epistemic Position & Authority
Who could define relevance, meaning, or direction?
04
Experiential Reorientation
Did collaborators come to sense the issue differently?
05
Evaluation Friction
Where did existing criteria fail to capture the work?
06
Context & Stage Sensitivity
When and under what conditions did the contribution become visible?

Consortia
Structural Implication

Why This Matters
for Consortia

When artistic research is included in transdisciplinary collaborations, it is too easily reduced to outreach, communication, or aesthetic framing.

→
That is not a neutral situation — it is a structural positioning that shapes what artistic research can actually do.
→
EMI is a way to name the role more precisely from the beginning — not only to evaluate it at the end.
→
The point is not that artistic research has value. The point is that it can participate in knowledge-making itself — and that this needs to be designed, not assumed.
